## Systematic Errors in Mathematics

### Open repository of alternative reasoning patterns and conceptual difficulties in K-12 mathematics

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Description

ESMa is an open repository dedicated to documenting, classifying, and
analyzing recurring patterns of mathematical reasoning that differ from
expected conventional mathematical knowledge.

**Systematic Errors in Mathematics (ESMa)** represent particular ways in
which students interpret, construct, and apply mathematical knowledge.
These patterns may appear logical from the student's perspective, but
they lead to conclusions that differ from mathematically accepted
knowledge within a specific context.

The purpose of ESMa is to provide an accessible knowledge base for
teachers, researchers, and educational communities, allowing them to
identify, understand, and address these conceptual difficulties through
evidence-based teaching strategies.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Purpose

ESMa aims to build an open knowledge base about students' mathematical
thinking, promoting mathematics education centered on a deeper
understanding of the cognitive processes involved in learning.

Its main objectives are:

-   Document systematic errors in mathematics from primary education
    through high school.
-   Classify recurring patterns of mathematical reasoning.
-   Provide teachers with tools to identify and address conceptual
    difficulties.
-   Promote collaboration and research in mathematics education.
-   Build a Spanish-based repository that enables knowledge exchange
    with educational communities worldwide.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Why "Systematic Errors"?

In ESMa, the term **systematic error** does not refer to an isolated
mistake or a lack of mathematical ability.

A systematic error is a recurring reasoning pattern that emerges when a
learner constructs a mathematical interpretation that is coherent from
their own perspective, but does not align with conventional mathematical
knowledge.

Understanding these patterns allows educators to move beyond simply
correcting incorrect answers and instead understand how students think
in order to design better learning experiences.

## Record Classification

Each ESMa record receives a unique code:

    ESMa-[Area]-[Topic]-[Number]

Example:

    ESMa-AR-JO-001

Where:

-   **ESMa** = Error Sistemático en Matemáticas (Systematic Errors in
    Mathematics).
-   **AR** = Mathematical area.
-   **JO** = Specific topic.
-   **001** = Sequential record number.

The original acronym is preserved to maintain the identity of the
initiative in Spanish while allowing international collaboration.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Initial Mathematical Areas

The initial taxonomy includes the following areas:

  Code   Area
  ------ -----------------------------------------------
  AR     Arithmetic
  FR     Fractions, ratios, and proportional reasoning
  AL     Algebra
  GE     Geometry
  MG     Magnitudes and measurement
  DA     Data and probability
  CA     Calculus
  LG     Logic and mathematical reasoning
  NT     Numbers and number theory
  VA     Mathematical visualization

This classification is designed to grow progressively from primary
education through high school and beyond.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Structure of Each ESMa Record

Each documented systematic error includes:

-   Unique code.
-   Name of the systematic error.
-   Mathematical area and topic.
-   Educational level where it commonly appears.
-   Description of the observed pattern.
-   Examples of student reasoning.
-   Mathematically accepted response.
-   Possible cognitive or instructional causes.
-   Suggested intervention strategies.
-   References and available evidence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Repository Philosophy

> Students' mathematical errors are not merely incorrect answers; they
> are evidence of how learners construct meaning about mathematical
> concepts.

By studying these patterns, educators and researchers can transform
learning difficulties into opportunities to improve mathematics
teaching.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Project Origin

ESMa originated as an educational documentation and dissemination
initiative derived from the project **"Gema Ordena: de jerarquía de
operaciones, potencias y raíces"**, with the purpose of expanding the
study of conceptual difficulties in mathematics and building an open
knowledge base in Spanish.

The repository will continue growing through the progressive
incorporation of new mathematical topics, areas, and educational
experiences.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Languages

The primary language of ESMa is Spanish, with the goal of strengthening
the creation and dissemination of mathematical education knowledge
within Spanish-speaking communities.

English versions are provided to facilitate international collaboration
and knowledge exchange with researchers and educators from around the
world.
